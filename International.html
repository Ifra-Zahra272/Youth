Purpose of International Youth Day:

Raising Awareness:

Bringing attention to the issues, challenges, and opportunities facing young people worldwide.

Celebrating Youth Contributions:

Recognizing the valuable role of youth in shaping a better future and celebrating their achievements.

Promoting Youth Engagement:

Encouraging active participation of young people in all aspects of society, including decision-making processes.

Providing a Platform for Voices:

Giving young people a voice to express their ideas and experiences and to be heard on issues important to them.

History and Background:

1999:

TheUN General Assembly designated August 12th as International Youth Day, following a recommendation from the World Conference of Ministers Responsible for Youth held in Lisbon in 1998.

1985:

The UN designated 1985 as the International Youth Year: Participation, Development, Peace, according to the UN.

1965:

The General Assembly endorsed the Declaration on the Promotion of Peace, Mutual Respect, and Understanding between Peoples, says the UN.

Key Themes and Activities:

Each year, International Youth Day adopts a theme to focus on a specific area of youth-related issues, according to UNESCO. Activities include concerts, workshops, cultural events, and meetings involving national and local government officials and youth organizations around the world. Events are organized to raise awareness about the situation of youth in their countries and to encourage young people to organize their own activities.

International Youth Work:

International youth work refers to collaborative efforts between countries to address youth issues and promote youth development. It involves exchanging experiences, building networks, and working together on shared goals related to youth empowerment and participation. It is often guided by international conventions like the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child and the EU Charter of Fundamental Rights, according to a document on International Youth Work.
